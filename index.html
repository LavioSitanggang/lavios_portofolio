KATAKAN AI: AI-Powered Virtual Meeting Accessibility

An award-winning accessibility platform designed to bridge communication gaps for deaf individuals in virtual meeting rooms, developed for the Microsoft AI for Accessibility (AI4A) Hackathon.

Computer Vision Speech-to-Text AI Natural Language Processiong Microsoft Azure

Challenge Identified

Deaf individuals face significant communication barriers in virtual meetings. Existing tools are often inadequate, lacking integrated, real-time features for Indonesian-language voice-to-text transcription, sign language interpretation, and contextual vocabulary support, which hinders seamless collaboration.

Strategic Role

As the Product & Vision Strategist, my primary contribution was defining the product's core "why" and "how." I was responsible for shaping the complete product vision, analyzing user needs, and designing the end-to-end business solution architecture. I focused on translating complex accessibility challenges into a feasible technical product roadmap, which was instrumental in guiding the team's development and securing victory (competing against 25 other teams)..

Technical Innovation

Architected a multi-modal solution deployable as a standalone desktop/mobile platform or as an integrated video-conferencing extension:

Voice-to-Text Engine : A real-time Indonesian speech-to-text transcription system, enabling deaf individuals to read the live conversation.
Sign Language Interpreter: A computer vision model that recognizes and translates sign language (hand gestures) into understandable text and/or speech.
Contextual NLP Chatbot: An integrated chatbot to provide definitions, synonyms, or examples for complex or uncommon vocabulary during the meeting.
Automatic Media Captioning An AI-powered auto-captioning feature for shared media (presentation slides, videos, etc.).

Measurable Impact

Achieved 1st Place in the Microsoft & Telkom University AI for Accessibility (AI4A) Hackathon. This victory secured our team Azure credits, dedicated technical support from Microsoft experts, and official development assistance to scale our idea into a real-world product on Microsoft Azure, with the potential to impact 22.5 million people with disabilities in Indonesia.
